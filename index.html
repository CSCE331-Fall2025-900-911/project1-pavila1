About Me

About Me

My name is Paola Avila, and I am a Computer Engineering student from Dallas, Texas, driven by a passion for the evolution of hardware in our society. Technology is constantly reshaping our world, and I am determined to be an active contributor to that change. As a proud first-generation Latina, I am on track to become the first computer engineer in my family, a milestone that fuels my dedication. This journey stems from a lifelong fascination with computers, which has now blossomed into a deeper, more nuanced understanding of the intricate systems that power our modern lives.

Why Computer Engineering?

My path to Computer Engineering was shaped by a dual passion for both software and hardware. While I was initially captivated by coding and the logic of developing programs, my interests expanded to include the physical world of electrical concepts. In high school, I fully immersed myself in electrical robotics, which provided the perfect environment to merge these two worlds. This experience was critical for honing my skills, challenging me to develop complex algorithms while simultaneously applying critical thinking to solve problems in electrical fields. For me, Computer Engineering is the ideal synthesis of these interests, allowing me to build the physical systems and write the code that brings them to life.

My Engineering Philosophy

For me, great engineering isn't just about creating the most powerful hardware; it's about designing technology that is accessible, efficient, and built to last. I believe in a 'first principles' approach to problem-solving—breaking a complex challenge down to its fundamental truths before building a solution. This was a lesson I learned firsthand during my time in robotics, where the most elegant and effective solutions were often the simplest and most robust.

Key Influences and Inspirations

My passion for hardware was significantly influenced by the development of embedded systems in everyday devices. Learning how engineers could pack so much computational power into a tiny, energy-efficient chip opened my eyes to a new world of possibilities. It showed me the power of combining visionary thinking with meticulous execution. This inspiration was further solidified by my coursework with on microprocessor architecture, which crystallized my desire to work at the intersection of hardware design and low-level software.

Collaboration and Working Style

In collaborative settings, I naturally gravitate towards the role of the systems integrator. I enjoy taking different components—whether they're lines of code from one teammate or a circuit designed by another—and figuring out how to make them work together seamlessly. My experience in robotics taught me that clear, consistent communication and a shared respect for each member's expertise are the bedrock of any successful project. I thrive in an environment where ideas can be challenged constructively to achieve the best possible outcome.

Hobbies and Interests

I do more than just work—I have hobbies that keep me balanced and inspired!

  • Running: Exploring new trails and pushing my limits, one step at a time.
  • Gardening: Cultivating a small garden, which teaches me patience and the rewards of careful planning.
  • Animation: I'm really into animation and appreciate the artistry behind it.
  • Gaming: Unwinding with strategic games that challenge my problem-solving skills in a fun way.
